# Evelyn Colon case-history claim register

Last reviewed: July 13, 2026

This repository note records the decisions behind historically sensitive wording in `index.html`; it is retained for maintenance and is not linked from the public page. Legal claims defer to the September 25, 2024, court opinion; forensic and identification claims prefer official police, NCMEC, and Othram material; family and burial history uses attributed reporting. Crowdsourced material supports only memorial and approximate-location details.

| Topic | Published wording or rule | Evidence and decision |
|---|---|---|
| Birth | “Born April 17, 1961; from Jersey City.” Do not state that Jersey City was her birthplace without a vital record. | Family and press accounts consistently place her in Jersey City but the reviewed public sources do not establish birthplace. |
| Age and death window | Age 15; estimated December 13–19, 1976. | [Othram case history](https://dnasolves.com/articles/beth-doe.html) and [Times News retrospective](https://www.tnonline.com/20210515/spotlight-identified-at-last/). December 19 is an investigative estimate, not a certain date. |
| Pathologist | Dr. Halbert E. Fillinger Jr. | [Othram case history](https://dnasolves.com/articles/beth-doe.html). Earlier page wording “Herbert J. Filinger” was corrected. |

| Discovery | Kenneth Jumper Jr., 14, found the remains near the Lehigh River below Interstate 80 on December 20, 1976. | [Othram case history](https://dnasolves.com/articles/beth-doe.html) and [Times News retrospective](https://www.tnonline.com/20210515/spotlight-identified-at-last/). Omit weakly sourced assumptions about vehicle direction, intent, and exact suitcase landing positions. |

| DNA timeline | New extract in November 2020; Othram returned the strong nephew match in February 2021; identity and charge public March 31; PSP briefing April 14. | [Othram case history](https://dnasolves.com/articles/beth-doe.html) and [PSP briefing](https://pacast.com/m?p=18678). The former “2020 DNA match” wording was inaccurate. |
| Paternity | Luis Sierra is identified by family accounts and charging records as the baby’s father. | [CNN via WBAL-TV](https://www.wbaltv.com/article/suspect-arrested-in-death-of-a-teenage-girl-who-disappeared-in-1976/36020737) and [Times News hearing report](https://www.tnonline.com/20210428/i-cant-sleep-at-night-beth-does-brother-testifies/). No reviewed source reports a public paternity test. |

There was no trial. |
| 2024 dismissal | Pennsylvania charge dismissed for lack of subject-matter jurisdiction. The court did not decide guilt, transfer the case, or conclusively adjudicate New Jersey jurisdiction. | [Carbon County Court memorandum opinion](https://www.carboncourts.com/opinions/jmatika/CommonwealthvsLuisSierra.pdf), September 25, 2024. | Cottingham theory | Identify it as a defense theory.
